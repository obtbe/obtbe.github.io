    <div class="bio-content">
      <p>I grew up in Bamako, Mali, captivated by numbers and nature. My grandad, a horse trainer, fueled that love. At his stable, filled with the scent of hay, he taught me to ride, showing how a small lean could guide a horse’s path. It was math woven into motion, and it still shapes my thinking.</p>
      <p>Today, I use data to solve problems. I founded Quantidum, a data analytics firm, to help businesses make clear decisions with tools like Python and SQL. Each client project teaches me something new. I also launched MansaPi, a non-profit to spark STEM passion in Mali’s kids. Through workshops, they code basic games or build solar-powered fans, discovering what’s possible.</p>
      <p>My blog explores data and tech ideas. I hope it lights a spark for you.</p>
      <blockquote class="quote">
        “Yesterday I was clever, so I wanted to change the world. Today I am wise, so I am changing myself.” — Rumi
