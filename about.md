    <p>As a kid, I was hooked on numbers and nature. My grandad, who loved horses, played a big role in that. We’d spend hours at his stable near Bamako, Mali, where the air smelled of hay and dust. He taught me to ride, but it was more than just sitting on a horse. He’d explain how a slight lean could guide the animal or how balance affected its stride. It was math in motion, not from a textbook, but alive in the rhythm of hoofbeats. Those lessons shaped how I think today.</p>

    <p>Now, I tackle uncertainty through data. I founded Quantidum, a data analytics firm that uses tools like Python and SQL to solve real problems for businesses. It’s a journey of constant growth, and I learn something new every project. But it’s not just about work. I’m passionate about giving back, so I started MansaPi, a non-profit in Mali to spark a love for STEM in kids. Through hands-on projects, like building simple circuits or coding games, we show students how science and math can change their world.</p>

    <p>This blog shares my thoughts on data, tech, and problem-solving. I hope you find it useful and inspiring.</p>
